Cigars

Our top recommended brands of Cigar

Cohiba

Normally the most expensive brand on average, Cohiba cigars were allegedly rolled specially for Fidel Castro. They were also given to visiting heads of state. These days anyone can buy one, usually for around $60-70 per piece, and the tobacco is extra fermented.

Partagas

Not for the faint of heart, these strong cigars pack a real punch. Take it slow to enjoy the earthy, peppery flavors without getting sick.

Bolivar

These strong cigars come with a warning for beginners, who should smoke them slowly due to a strong tobacco taste. If you start off to fast you might get sick or flush, but those who can handle them will enjoy tangy, earthy flavors. The Royal Corona is particularly feted.

Coffee

The only coffee you will ever need

Bustelo Supreme Whole Bean Espresso Coffee

This brand has been around for almost 100 years! Bustelo is a brand that’s always received several accolades, even earning first place in several international coffee competitions.

Mayorga Cuban Coffee Whole Bean Coffee

This Cuban coffee has a robust flavor. It’s characterized by dark yet bright beans that have quite a syrupy texture. If you’re interested in trying this coffee, beware! Mayorga’s blends are pretty strong, so they might be a bit harsh for beginners.

La Llave Coffee Cuban Espresso

This coffee has a sweet, chocolate aroma that makes for the perfect start to the day. It’s also got quite a high caffeine content, so it’s perfect for adding pep to your step on sleepy mornings.
